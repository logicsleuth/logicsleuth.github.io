OwlEye is an independent cybersecurity blog. The name is a nod to the wide-eyed watching that security work demands — and to the fact that most breaches are visible long before they're exploited, if you know where to look.

This site exists to publish research that is useful, accurate, and actionable. Every post is written by hands-on practitioners: CTF players, reverse engineers, detection engineers, and sysadmins who got curious about a bug and followed it to the root cause.

What we publish

  • CTF writeups — full walkthroughs with the dead ends included, not just the flag.
  • Threat analysis — breakdowns of real-world malware, phishing campaigns, and exploit chains.
  • Tool deep-dives — when existing tools aren't enough and you need to read the source.
  • Defensive guides — detection rules, hardening checklists, and post-incident playbooks.

Responsible disclosure

We follow coordinated disclosure. If you're a vendor and something on this site touches one of your products, reach out — we'll work with you to confirm, reproduce, and remediate before anything is published.
